**Product:** Coconut-shell charcoal briquettes for shisha/hookah · **Origin:** Indonesia · **POL:** Semarang / Tanjung Emas (IDSRG) · **DG:** UN 1361, IMDG Class 4.2, SHT provided · **Date checked:** 2026-06-21

*Rules of evidence applied throughout. Labels: (a)=what the regulation says · (b)=carrier requirement · (c)=practice. Destination customs clearance is the BUYER's responsibility — the factory exports and documents only. No per-ton charcoal price is published; landed-cost layers are illustrative percentages only. "UNVERIFIED" flags an unconfirmed item; a flagged unknown is intended, a guess is not.*

## TL;DR / DECISION SUMMARY
1. **Classify as 4402.20.00.00 ("of shells or nuts") in all four markets, never 4402.90 or 4401.** Duty is **0% in the UK and EU/Germany**, **5% in Saudi Arabia (GCC CET)** and **5% in Russia (EAEU CCT)** — Indonesia earns **no usable duty preference anywhere** (UK/EU MFN already 0%; Indonesia removed from EU GSP and EAEU preferences; GCC has none).
2. **Two hard non-tariff gates dominate feasibility:** (i) **EU — EUDR**: 4402 charcoal IS in Annex I scope; the EU importer must file a Due Diligence Statement (geolocation + deforestation-free + legality), applying **30 Dec 2026** (large/medium) / **30 Jun 2027** (micro/small). (ii) **Saudi — SABER**: a Shipment Certificate of Conformity is mandatory for clearance. SFDA tobacco rules and Saudi excise do **not** touch charcoal.
3. **Russia is materially constrained, not routine:** Western container lines suspended Russia bookings (1 Mar 2022); DG Class 4.2 acceptance to Russian ports is carrier-specific and **UNVERIFIED**; major Russian banks are off SWIFT; and Russian import **VAT rose to 22% from 1 Jan 2026**. Confirm a DG-accepting carrier and a sanctions-clear payment channel before quoting.

## UNITED KINGDOM

### [UK · C1] — Commodity code
**Finding (a):** Coconut-shell charcoal classifies under heading 4402, subheading 4402 20 ("Of shells or nuts"); the full UK declarable commodity code is **4402 20 00 00**. GOV.UK "Classifying wood" confirms heading 4402 "includes charcoal obtained by carbonising shells and nuts, and charcoal agglomerated with tar." 4402.90 ("Other") is wrong because General Rule of Interpretation 3(a) gives precedence to the more specific "of shells or nuts" subheading.
**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/guidance/classifying-wood ; UK Integrated Online Tariff https://www.trade-tariff.service.gov.uk/ (gov tool, checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High
**Notes:** Declare 4402.20.00.00 + UN 1361 Class 4.2. Do NOT declare as 3802 (activated carbon) or 4401 (fuel wood) — both are factually wrong for carbonised coconut-shell briquettes and create seizure/penalty risk.

### [UK · C2] — Import duty
**Finding (a):** UK Global Tariff (UKGT) third-country/MFN duty for 4402 is **0%** from most origins. Indonesia is eligible under the **Developing Countries Trading Scheme (DCTS)** "Standard Preferences" tier. Per GOV.UK "Preference tiers under the Developing Countries Trading Scheme": *"There are 2 countries eligible for Standard Preferences (India and Indonesia)… Standard Preferences entitle exporters in eligible countries to 0% import tariffs on 65% of product lines, while a further 26% of product lines have reduced tariffs."* Because MFN is already 0%, the DCTS preference confers no additional duty benefit for this code. On goods graduation: per the GOV.UK/HMRC DCTS graduation notice, preferential rates are suspended **from 1 January 2026 to 31 December 2028**, and for Indonesia the graduated chapters cover **oil-based products, footwear and musical instruments** (not charcoal) — so charcoal is unaffected by graduation. No anti-dumping/trade-remedy on Indonesian charcoal found.
**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/guidance/preference-tiers-under-the-developing-countries-trading-scheme ; GOV.UK DCTS goods graduation notice / government policy response (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High
**Notes:** As-of UKGT 2026. Confirm the live MFN line on trade-tariff.service.gov.uk each shipment.

### [UK · C3] — Import VAT
**Finding (a):** Import VAT is **20%** (standard rate), charged on customs value + duty + freight/insurance to UK port. Postponed VAT Accounting (PVA) lets VAT-registered importers account for import VAT on the VAT return rather than paying at the border; import VAT is then recoverable as input tax under normal rules.
**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/guidance/tariffs-on-goods-imported-into-the-uk ; GOV.UK PVA guidance (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High
**Notes:** Buyer needs a GB EORI and (for PVA) VAT registration.

### [UK · C4] — Other charges
**Finding (c):** No charcoal-specific national levy. Standard third-party costs apply: port/terminal handling, carrier DG surcharges, customs broker fees, and any Border Force/HMRC examination charges. These are commercial, not government tariffs.
**Source:** UNVERIFIED published rates — needs forwarder/port schedule.
**Confidence:** Low

### [UK · C5] — Entry & security filing
**Finding (a/b):** A Safety & Security declaration (ENS / S&S GB) is required for goods arriving into GB; the import declaration is filed via CDS; RoRo/port movements use GVMS for a Goods Movement Reference. The carrier/operator is responsible for S&S; the importer/agent files the customs declaration.
**Source:** GOV.UK S&S GB and GVMS guidance (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** Medium
**Notes:** Filing is the buyer/importer's responsibility; the Indonesian factory supplies export documents only.

### [UK · C6] — Regulatory / conformity
**Finding (a):** **UK REACH** applies to substances/articles; no charcoal-specific UK REACH restriction identified — rely on SDS. **UK Timber Regulation (UKTR, assimilated Reg. 995/2010, enforced by OPSS):** the UKTR enforcement report (2022–2025) lists HS codes actually checked (4401, 4407, 4409, 4412) and does **not** list 4402 charcoal; the "wood fuel" workstream references firewood/logs/chips/pellets/briquettes (4401-type). Carbonised charcoal (4402) is therefore **not clearly in UKTR/FLEGT scope**. Indonesia is the only country with a FLEGT VPA with the UK, so genuinely in-scope timber from Indonesia needs a FLEGT licence — but coconut-shell charcoal is an agricultural byproduct (*Cocos nucifera*), not timber.
**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/timber-regulations-reports/uktr-report-2022-to-2025 ; https://www.gov.uk/guidance/regulations-timber-and-flegt-licences (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** Medium
**Notes / needs confirmation:** Confirm with OPSS that 4402 coconut-shell charcoal is out of UKTR/FLEGT scope before relying on exemption. General product-safety rules apply at retail.

### [UK · C7] — DG arrival handling
**Finding (a/b):** UN 1361 (carbon of animal/vegetable origin) is IMDG Class 4.2, "substances liable to spontaneous combustion." IMDG Amendment 42-24 introduced Special Provision **SP 978**, which removes the former SP 925/SP 223 exemptions — voluntary from 1 Jan 2025, **mandatory from 1 Jan 2026**. SP 978 requires: declaration as DG with no exemption; material weathered ≥14 days in open air (or steam/inert-gas treated then ≥24h under cover); packed only when material ≤40°C; PG III minimum without testing; ventilation/stowage per stowage codes (SW1/SW27; EmS F-A, S-J). UK ports apply IMDG internationally; no UK-only hazmat rule identified at arrival.
**Source:** https://www.burgoynes.com/Media/pdf/charcoal-carriage-by-jim-mercurio.pdf ; https://britanniapandi.com/2025/01/safe-carriage-of-charcoal-in-containers/ (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High (IMDG); Low (UK national port specifics)
**Notes:** Carriers increasingly book coconut charcoal as DG regardless of test result; SHT + weathering report should accompany the shipment.

### [UK · C8] — Document set
**Finding:** B/L; commercial invoice; packing list; certificate of origin (DCTS origin declaration on invoice if claiming preference — duty is 0% MFN anyway, old GSP Form A no longer accepted); MSDS/SDS; DG declaration (UN 1361/4.2); SHT/weathering report; ISPM-15 treated pallets.
**Source:** Composite of GOV.UK import guidance + IMDG (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** Medium

### [UK · C9] — Ports + LOCODEs
**Finding:** Felixstowe **GBFXT**; London Gateway **GBLGP**; Southampton **GBSOU**.
**Source:** UN/LOCODE (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** Medium
**Notes:** Verify LOCODE strings against the UNECE list.

### [DE/EU · C6] — Regulatory / conformity — EUDR (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Finding (a) — EUDR:** HS heading **4402 charcoal IS in scope** of the EU Deforestation Regulation (Reg. (EU) 2023/1115) — Annex I lists "4402 Wood charcoal (including shell or nut charcoal)." Reg. (EU) **2025/2650** (published in the EU Official Journal 23 Dec 2025) postponed application to: **30 December 2026 for large/medium operators; 30 June 2027 for micro/small operators.** The EU importer (first operator placing the product on the EU market) must submit a **Due Diligence Statement (DDS)** via the EU Information System (TRACES), demonstrating geolocation of production plots, deforestation-free status (land not deforested after 31 Dec 2020) and legality. A Commission simplification review was due by 30 April 2026; a draft delegated act on product scope was open for public feedback until 1 June 2026. Micro/small primary operators may submit a one-off simplified declaration; downstream non-SME operators no longer file their own DDS but must retain upstream DDS references.
**Finding (a) — EU REACH + CLP:** apply to substances/mixtures; rely on SDS; no charcoal-specific restriction identified.
**Finding (a) — EUTR (predecessor):** the EU Timber Regulation continues transitionally for certain timber harvested before 29 Jun 2023; EUDR supersedes it.
**Source:** EUDR scope/Annex I https://environment.ec.europa.eu/topics/forests/deforestation/regulation-deforestation-free-products_en ; delay https://trade.ec.europa.eu/access-to-markets/en/news/delay-until-december-2026-and-other-developments-implementation-eudr-regulation ; https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20251211IPR32168/ (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High
**Notes / DECISION ITEM:** Although coconut shell is arguably an agricultural byproduct (not a forest tree), Annex I lists 4402 with no carve-out, so **EUDR applies**. The factory must be able to supply geolocation + deforestation-free + legality evidence so its EU buyer can file the DDS. Correct 4402.20 classification (vs 4402.90) reduces classification-trigger friction but does **not** remove EUDR scope.

### [SA · C6] — Regulatory / conformity (SASO/SABER/SFDA/excise)
**Finding (a) — SASO/SABER:** Every shipment requires a SABER Shipment Certificate of Conformity (SCoC) to clear customs; shipments without an SCoC are refused/re-exported. SABER determines regulation status by HS code. Whether 4402 charcoal is a **"regulated"** product (needing a Product CoC under a named SASO Technical Regulation) or **"non-regulated"** (needing only a Self-Declaration of Conformity plus the per-shipment SCoC) was **not confirmed** for HS 4402 in this research.
**Finding (a) — SFDA:** SFDA jurisdiction under the Anti-Smoking Law covers tobacco and waterpipe tobacco molasses (mu'assel), defined via tobacco/its derivatives. **Shisha charcoal is a heat source, not tobacco**, so it falls outside SFDA tobacco controls.
**Finding (a) — Excise/selective tax:** Saudi selective/excise tax applies to tobacco (100%), soft drinks (50%), energy/sweetened drinks (100%) — **NOT charcoal**. Confirmed: no excise on charcoal (as expected).
**Source:** SASO/SABER (Intertek; GraniteRiverLabs); SFDA tobacco scope https://www.tobaccocontrollaws.org/legislation/saudi-arabia ; excise https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/saudi-arabia-import-tariffs (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High (SFDA exclusion; excise = NO); Medium (SASO regulated-vs-non-regulated status — DECISION ITEM)
**Notes / DECISION ITEM:** Confirm in SABER whether 4402.20 is regulated (PCoC required, valid 1 year) or non-regulated (Self-Declaration + per-shipment SCoC). Arabic labelling applies. G-Mark/Saleem framework is relevant only if a technical regulation actually captures charcoal.

## RUSSIA (EAEU)

### [RU · C1] — Commodity code
**Finding (a):** ТН ВЭД ЕАЭС code **4402 20 000 0** ("из скорлупы или орехов" / of shells or nuts) for coconut-shell charcoal; **4402 90 000 0** = "прочий" (other). ### [RU · C2] — Import duty
**Finding (a):** EAEU Common Customs Tariff (ЕТТ) MFN base duty = **5%** for 4402 90 000 0 (confirmed via Alta-Soft); the 4402 group carries the same 5% base rate (4402 20 line item not independently confirmed). **Indonesia was REMOVED from the EAEU unified system of tariff preferences (developing-country beneficiary list) effective 12 October 2021** (EEC Council Decision No. 17 of 5 Mar 2021), cutting the list from 103 to 29 countries. The former preference (25% reduction = 75% of base for developing countries) therefore **does NOT apply — full 5% MFN duty applies.** No anti-dumping found.
**Source:** https://www.alta.ru/tnved/code/4402900000/ ; EEC tariff preferences https://eec.eaeunion.org/comission/department/dotp/tariff_preferences.php (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High (5% for 4402 90; Indonesia GSP removal); Medium (5% for 4402 20 — re-confirm line item on alta.ru/FCS)

### [RU · C3] — Import VAT (НДС)
**Finding (a):** Russian standard VAT was **20% in 2025** and was **raised to 22% from 1 January 2026**. This is confirmed: Federal Law No. 425-FZ (draft law No. 1026190-8) was **signed by the President on 28 November 2025**; per Sovos and Orbitax the standard rate rose from 20% to 22% effective 1 January 2026, while *"the reduced rate of 10% is retained for essential goods like food, medicines, and children's products."* Charcoal does not qualify for the 10%/0% rates, so import VAT = **22% (2026)** on (customs value + duty). Registered VAT-paying importers can deduct import VAT (Arts. 171–172 NK RF; within 3 years, with customs declaration + proof of payment).
**Source:** Federal Law No. 425-FZ (ConsultantPlus) https://www.consultant.ru/legalnews/30257/ ; Sovos / Orbitax; deduction https://www.garant.ru/pravovest-audit-sp/nds-import-tovarov.html (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** High
**Notes:** This supersedes the brief's 20% assumption: from 2026-01-01 the rate is **22%**.

### [RU · C6] — Regulatory / conformity + practical feasibility (PRIORITY)
**Finding (a) — EAC/GOST:** Charcoal (incl. coconut-shell charcoal fuel) is **not subject to mandatory EAC certification or declaration** under EAEU technical regulations in the general case; importers typically obtain an **"отказное письмо"** (negative/refusal letter) confirming no certificate is required, useful at customs. **GOST 7657-84** ("Уголь древесный. Технические условия") exists but compliance is **voluntary**. Contested point: some Russian certification agencies claim a declaration under **TR TS 021/2011 (food safety)** may apply IF charcoal is intended for food/grill cooking — shisha charcoal is a fuel, so generally N/A. These are commercial certification-agency sources and partly contradict each other → flagged.
**Finding (c) — Carrier feasibility:** Major Western container lines (Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, ONE) suspended new Russia bookings **effective 1 March 2022**; per the Maersk market update, *"new Maersk bookings within ocean, air, and intercontinental rail to and from Russia will be temporarily suspended, with the exception of foodstuffs, medical and humanitarian supplies"* (Hapag-Lloyd had already halted Russia/Ukraine bookings earlier, on 24 February 2022). General cargo to St Petersburg (RULED), Novorossiysk (RUNVS) and especially **Vladivostok (RUVVO)** now moves via regional/neutral/Asian carriers and transshipment hubs (China/Türkiye/UAE); Vladivostok is the most practical routing for Indonesian-origin cargo. **DG Class 4.2 acceptance by these carriers to Russian ports is UNVERIFIED and carrier-specific.**
**Finding (c) — Banking/payment:** Major Russian banks have been cut from SWIFT since March 2022 (expanded through 2025–2026); payments require non-sanctioned banks, local-currency settlement (RUB/IDR/CNY), intermediary banks (China/UAE/Central Asia) or alternative messaging (SPFS/CIPS). The **EU 20th sanctions package** (entered into force 24 April 2026) added — for the first time — a third-country port to the restricted list, the **Karimun Oil Terminal in Indonesia**, subject to a transaction ban, signalling sanctions reach into Indonesia–Russia logistics.
**Source:** EAC https://optimatest.ru/sertifikaciya-uglya/ ; GOST 7657-84; carrier suspensions https://gcaptain.com/worlds-largest-shipping-lines-suspend-shipping-to-russia/ + Maersk update; sanctions (Ropes & Gray / Crowell & Moring, 20th package eif 24 Apr 2026) (checked 2026-06-21)
**Confidence:** Medium-High (EAC/GOST; carrier suspension; banking general); Low (DG acceptance; specific payment routes; TR TS 021/2011 applicability)
**Notes / DECISION ITEM:** Russia feasibility is materially constrained — confirm BEFORE quoting: (1) a carrier that will accept UN 1361 Class 4.2 to the target Russian port, and (2) a compliant, sanctions-clear payment channel.

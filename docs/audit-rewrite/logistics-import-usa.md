## 3. Targeted rewrites

Only genuine gaps below. Everything not listed already passes — do not touch it.

### 3a. Rule 1 — Headings as buyer questions

The featured-snippet leads are already answer-shaped, so converting the H2 labels to questions is low-risk and lifts AI/snippet capture. No facts change.

**`hts.h2`** — current: `"HTS classification"`
Corrected: `"What HTS code is coconut charcoal under in the USA?"`

**`duty.h2`** — current: `"Duties & fees"`
Corrected: `"What duty and fees apply when importing to the USA?"`

**`landed.h2`** — current: `"Landed-cost worked example"`
Corrected: `"What is the landed cost of a container to the USA?"`

**`entry.h2`** — current: `"The entry process"`
Corrected: `"How do I clear a UN 1361 container through US customs?"`
*(Note: `entry.h2` also feeds the HowTo schema `name`. The question form is acceptable as a HowTo name; if a cleaner imperative is preferred, `"How to clear a UN 1361 container through US customs"` keeps it a valid HowTo title while staying question-adjacent.)*

**`ports.h2`** — current: `"US ports & routing"`
Corrected: `"Which US ports do you ship to, and how long is transit?"`

*(Leave `countryAgency.h2` as-is — it is a descriptive section label already paired with a question-form intro and the FAQ owns the literal "Is hookah charcoal FDA-regulated?" question. Leave `responsibility.h2` "Who handles what" — it is already an implicit question and reads well as a label. Leave `dgHandling.h2` and `leadTime.h2` — both fine.)*

### 3b. Rule 4 — Anti-bloat: intro meta-sentence

The middle clause speaks to site maintainers, not buyers, and adds no import information.

Current (`intro.p1`):
> "This page sits under the {{brand}} Logistics pillar and is the template for our import-by-country guides. It covers the customs mechanics; the commercial case for the US market lives on the market page."

Corrected (`intro.p1`):
> "This page sits under the {{brand}} Logistics pillar and covers the US customs mechanics for coconut charcoal — classification, duty, entry, and FDA. The commercial case for the US market lives on the market page."

*(Keeps the pillar up-link and the market-page hand-off, drops the internal "template for our guides" note, and adds the four-topic scope cue that helps both buyers and snippet extraction. The trailing `hubLinkLabel` / `marketsLinkLabel` links render after this paragraph and still apply.)*

### 3c. Rule 4 — Minor: HTS body / FAQ-1 overlap (optional, low priority)

`hts.body` and `faq.items[0].a` repeat the "both Free base duty / CBP binding ruling / confirm with broker" point. This is *intended* under the one-Q&A-per-page FAQPage design (the FAQ answer must stand alone), so this is flagged, not failed. If trimming is desired, shorten the body's closing rather than the FAQ:

Current (`hts.body`):
> "…so the exact code does not change the base rate. A CBP binding-ruling request would settle it — confirm classification with your customs broker."

Optional tightened tail:
> "…so the exact code does not change the base rate. Confirm the final classification with your customs broker (a CBP binding ruling settles it definitively)."

No new facts; same two facts, less restating. Keep the FAQ answer unchanged.

## 5. Ontological completeness & triangulation

- **Genesis — explicit and strong.** Factory-as-source is anchored throughout via `{{port}}` / FOB Tanjung Emas (IDSRG), the responsibility split ("we export and document"), and the SP 978 weathering report. No gap.
- **Taxonomy — explicit and dense.** Coconut shell charcoal is correctly positioned: material class → HTS heading 4402 (4402.20.00.00 "of shell or nut" vs 4402.90.01.00 "other") → UN 1361 Class 4.2 dangerous goods → FDA "component or part of a tobacco product." This is the page's strongest dimension.
- **Pragmatics — explicit.** Buyer value is concrete at every step: who clears, what each fee costs, what documents the factory supplies, what compliance the buyer owns. No abstraction without a payoff.
- **Weak connectivity to close (semantic gap):** the **AD/CVD** node (`adcvd`: "No order found for Chapter 44 charcoal from Indonesia") is an isolated reassurance — it is not referenced from the duty lead or the at-a-glance box, so a skimming buyer may miss that there is *no* anti-dumping exposure. Consider one clause in the duty lead, e.g. "(no anti-dumping or countervailing-duty order applies — see below)" to wire it into the cost cluster. Facts already on-page; no new data.
- **Weak connectivity (minor):** the **Section 301 investigation opened 11 March 2026** mentioned in `dutyHistory` is a live tail-risk that only appears inside the history block; it could be surfaced in the FAQ-2 answer (which already discusses the volatile layer) so it is extractable. Optional; fact already on-site in `dutyHistory`.
- **Triangulation verdict:** all three vertices (factory-as-source, charcoal-taxonomy position, buyer-pragmatic value) are individually explicit and mutually linked. The only loose threads are the two isolated risk nodes above — both fixable with on-site facts, neither a fabrication risk.

Every shipment from {{legalName}} ships FOB {{port}}, in 20ft container loads against a buyer-issued Proforma Invoice. Goods are not returnable — return-shipping a 20ft container of charcoal across an ocean is commercially and environmentally untenable. Quality and quantity issues are resolved through the inspection and claims procedure below.

## Inspection on receipt

The buyer inspects the container within {{damageBusinessDays}} business days of arrival at the destination port. Before the seal is broken, the buyer photographs the container number plate, the unbroken seal, and the four exterior sides of the container. These photos are part of the evidentiary record for any later claim and for the buyer's marine cargo insurer.

Acceptable claim categories at this stage are: short shipment, transit damage, packaging damage, off-spec moisture, and off-spec ash content.

Required evidence at the time of filing:

- Container number and B/L number
- Master-carton lot or batch numbers from the affected cartons
- Photographs of the seal in place and after breaking
- Photographs of damaged or off-spec goods, with the carton lot number visible in frame
- A written narrative description of the issue

Open questions on documentation can be raised through [Contact](/contact) before the claim is formally submitted.

## Quality claim window

Quality and quantity claims must be filed within {{qualityDays}} days of the buyer receiving the goods. After the {{qualityDays}}-day window closes, the goods are deemed accepted and no further claims will be considered.

## Quality testing procedure

Sampling: random, drawn across the full container — not from a single pallet — at a minimum of {{samplingPercent}}% of the consignment by weight.

Moisture: tested per {{moistureStandard}}. The manufacturer guarantees moisture at or below {{moistureMaxPercent}}% at the time of loading.

Ash content is guaranteed by product tier:

Other parameters — calorific value, burn time, density, and similar — are tested only on prior written mutual agreement of both parties, with the test method, lab, and acceptance thresholds fixed in writing before sampling begins.

Lab: any laboratory engaged for a disputed test must be accredited by {{labAccreditor}} ({{labAccreditorFull}}, {{labCountry}}) to {{labStandard}}. Results from non-accredited labs are not admissible for the purposes of this policy.

Natural variation: coconut shells are an agricultural input, not a synthetic material. Drift of up to {{naturalVariationPercent}}% from the stated standard is accepted and is not, on its own, grounds for a claim.

For the underlying testing methodology and the full guaranteed specification ranges, see [Quality](/quality).

## Resolution

The default remedy for a sustained claim is a credit applied to the next container shipment, taken either as a discount on the goods or as additional units in that container. Credit is the operating norm because both parties want the relationship to continue.

A cash refund is offered only when no further orders are planned, and only after both parties confirm the resolution in writing.

Direct exchanges of off-spec product for in-spec product are not offered. If a different product is required, the buyer places a new order against a new Proforma Invoice.

## Exclusions

The following are not covered by this policy:

- Damage caused by storage conditions at the destination after the goods leave the port
- Damage caused by the buyer re-packaging the goods or co-mingling them with other lots
- Claims filed after the {{qualityDays}}-day quality window or the {{damageBusinessDays}}-business-day damage window
- Goods that have left the destination port for any onward leg of the supply chain

## Force majeure

Delays or damage caused by events beyond the manufacturer's reasonable control — port strikes, weather events, customs holds, vessel diversion, blank sailings — are not grounds for refund or credit under this policy. The manufacturer will assist with whatever export-side documentation the buyer's marine cargo insurer requires to process its own claim. The buyer is expected to carry marine cargo insurance on every shipment; the operational rationale for that requirement is set out under [Logistics](/logistics).

- The Proforma Invoice or order number
- The B/L number
- The container number
- Photographs per the inspection section above

- A written narrative of the issue and the remedy sought

Acknowledgement is sent within one business day in {{country}} (GMT+7). Resolution timelines depend on the claim category and the time required to receive lab results.
